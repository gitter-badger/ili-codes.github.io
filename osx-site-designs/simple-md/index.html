Mac OS X El Capitan

Revolutionary.

Macintosh is famously known for its easy-to-use interface and it pioneered the graphical interface in 1984 with System 0.1. With System 0.1, people didn’t have to type in commands anymore. They could use a “mouse” to click on things. It was revolutionary. So revolutionary, in fact, that Microsoft decided to copy Macintosh’s interface with Windows 1.01, released in 1985. That’s how the Graphical Interface was made. 32 years later, Macintosh is still the leader in graphical interface design, thanks to Jony Ive and his design team. The Mac’s interface is revolutionary easy, especially with Mac OS X El Capitan, the latest Mac OS (at the time of writing). Split-screen apps, the Dock, Launchpad, Notification Center, Finder, and more give Mac OS that easy look.

Powerful.

Deep down, it’s more powerful than it looks. Powered by a UNIX core, the same stuff that makes up servers, it’s blazing fast and very powerful. It can be used to check mail or edit the code to the app that will cure cancer. Either way, Mac OS is very easy-to-use and very powerful. Maintenance is taken care of already, so no need for registry edits or disk defragments. All you need to worry about is getting your work done.

And more.

Mac OS also allows for easy searching. Just press Option-Space and you’ll get Spotlight, a text box that can calculate, search, open, define, and more. OS X also comes with Handoff and Continuity, features that allow you to use apps on your Mac (example Google Chrome) with your iPhone, syncing progress, URLs, saves, and more. If you open Safari on Mac and shop on Amazon, and you open Safari on iOS, it'll sync all your tabs, carts, and progress straight to your iOS device.
