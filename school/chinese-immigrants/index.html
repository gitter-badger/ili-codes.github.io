Notes

  • The Canadian Pacific Railway went from the Atlantic Ocean to the Pacific Ocean
  • There wasn't a lot of farmland in China so Chinese immigrants loved the idea of going to a new country and getting farmland.
  • Other reasons to immigrate were war, floods, growing population, droughts, and high taxes.
  • Lots of men came and immigrated to Canada because of lots of job opportunities.
  • Building the railroad was very hard business. It was very cold.
  • European railway workers thought that Chinese workers took away their jobs.
  • Chinese workers got paid less than European workers.
  • To earn extra money, Chinese workers had to do dangerous jobs like blowing up things. Many died and their families were never told.
  • Soon, Chinese workers were not needed in Canada but they could not get back home to China.
  • A tax was made for only Chinese immigrants that was raised from $50 to $500 in about 20 years.
  • The tax stopped in 1923.
  • In 2006, the Government finally apologized to Chinese Canadians for the injustice that they did them.
